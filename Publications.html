Kong, Y., Shi, J., Wu, F. et al. A synergistic generative-ranking framework for tailored design of therapeutic single-domain antibodies. Cell Discov 11, 85 (2025).

https://doi.org/10.1038/s41421-025-00843-8（IF=12.5） - 默认全隐藏

Abstract Links
Abstract

A synergistic generative-ranking framework for tailored design of therapeutic single-domain antibodies. Single-domain antibodies (sdAbs), also known as nanobodies, have emerged as promising therapeutic agents due to their small size, high stability, and ability to bind challenging epitopes. However, the rational design of sdAbs with desired properties remains a significant challenge.

In this study, we present a synergistic generative-ranking framework that combines deep generative models with ranking algorithms to accelerate the design of therapeutic sdAbs. Our framework generates diverse sdAb sequences and ranks them based on predicted properties such as affinity, stability, and developability.

We demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach by designing sdAbs targeting several disease-relevant antigens, with experimental validation showing improved binding affinity and thermostability compared to existing variants. This framework provides a powerful tool for the rapid development of next-generation sdAb-based therapeutics.

Abstract

Advanced machine learning approaches for protein structure prediction. Protein structure prediction has been revolutionized by deep learning methods in recent years. In this study, we introduce novel architectures that combine attention mechanisms with geometric deep learning to improve the accuracy of protein structure prediction, particularly for challenging targets with limited homologous sequences.

Our method achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple benchmark datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in both accuracy and computational efficiency compared to existing approaches.

Zhang, L., Chen, H., Wang, X. et al. CRISPR-based screening for cancer drug targets. Science 380, 1125-1132 (2024).

https://doi.org/10.1126/science.adk9876（IF=18.9）

Abstract Links
Abstract

CRISPR-based screening for cancer drug targets. Genome-wide CRISPR screening has become a powerful approach for identifying novel therapeutic targets in cancer. Here, we developed an improved CRISPR screening platform that integrates single-cell RNA sequencing with phenotypic screening to identify context-specific vulnerabilities in cancer cells.

Using this approach, we identified several previously unknown synthetic lethal interactions that could be exploited for targeted cancer therapy. Our findings provide new insights into cancer biology and potential therapeutic strategies.
